Current Area of Interest : I am interested in user generated content and intellectual property rights.

On today's UGC platforms, users are credited for comments, photos, product reviews, etc. Corporations are the ones who profit from user contributions, indefinitely, providing almost zero compensation to their users. And corporations aren't on the hook for governance and free speech. Social networks go so far to beg/ask/incentivize users to monitor their platforms for fraud by flagging content and bots.

This benevolent kind of self-contained system worked in the good old days. Today things are different. There's a plague of bad actors, and things are getting worse fast with generative AI and bots.

Would the network effect be achievable without destruction of IP rights in a liberated system?

Would social networks differ if users had full and perpetual rights to control their own data? Today's tangled process is one where companies must comply with laws to grant users the right to be forgotten. Both sides must jump through legal and technical challenges to achieve this, generally lacking fine-grained embedded controls, in order to comply with the laws as they are written.

Fast Forward. In 2026, it seems like it should be possible for users to manage their own data through technology. Technology that can provide both portability and dual-sided accountability when it comes to UGC and PII.

I've been trying to understand if businesses and individuals can both be assured that submitted data is real without having to expend capital on fraud detection.

New technology could make it easier and cheaper to distinguish real people from bots and fake accounts.

For now, I am calling it "Anonymous Verified Users". By leveraging aspects of the DID spec, a blockchain ledger, zero knowledge proofs, and other technology to 1. reduce the cost of fraud detection, 2. provide fine-grain control over UGC, and 3. allow corporations to analyze user data without exposing user data.

Anonymous Verified Users

    We should be able to
  1. Share data
  2. Define how long we want to grant data access
  3. Retain ownership of our data
  4. Sign our data, in a way that is anonymous and unforgeable

AVU data consumers are entites with rights too.

    They can
  1. Have proof that data is REAL, not a bot, nor a forgery
  2. Perform computations on the data, without seeing the raw data

Currently, this system sounds and looks good, but one problem is that anyone with access to the data can copy the data in place to de-associate the owner from their data. The only defence is that if a bad actor copies data, they lose the provenance. But Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) and ZKP can lock down the gap.
